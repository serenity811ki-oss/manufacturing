# Executive Insights — Manufacturing Downtime, Jan 2023 – Dec 2024

## Headline

Across 4,200 logged downtime events and 4,330 downtime hours over two years, unplanned failures
account for **72.5%** of all events and drove **€2.05M** in repair spend. Plant Munich is the
largest single contributor to downtime hours, and material shortages — not mechanical wear — are
the single costliest downtime category. This points to a supply-chain and preventive-maintenance
opportunity rather than a machine-reliability crisis alone.

## Four headline findings

### 1. Worst performing plant: Plant Munich
1,639.9 downtime hours — the highest of the 4 plants and roughly 38% of total downtime hours
company-wide. Munich has more production lines (4) than any other site, so part of this is
scale, but its downtime-hours-per-line still outpaces Rotterdam and Lyon, making it the right
starting point for a root-cause deep dive.

### 2. Highest repair-cost machine: MC-0003 (Injection Molding Machine, Plant Munich)
€76,403 cumulative repair cost over the two-year window — more than double the next machine on
the list. A single asset carrying that much of the maintenance budget is a strong candidate for
either replacement-cost analysis or a dedicated predictive-maintenance sensor package.

### 3. Most common downtime reason: Product Changeover
211 logged events, more than any single mechanical or electrical failure mode. Because
changeover is a *planned* downtime driver, this is really an opportunity, not a risk — a SMED
(Single-Minute Exchange of Die) style time study could shrink planned downtime without touching
capital budget.

### 4. Highest downtime months: January 2023, December 2023, February 2023
Winter months consistently run 15–25% higher downtime hours than summer months across both
years, consistent with cold-start mechanical stress and higher electrical fault rates.

## Root cause summary

- **Material shortage is the #1 cost driver** at €484,667 — ahead of mechanical failure
  (€362,259) and electrical failure (€253,410). This means the biggest lever on total repair
  spend is supplier reliability and buffer-stock policy, not equipment condition.
- **Unplanned share is uniformly high across all 4 plants** (71.4%–73.8%), so the planned/
  unplanned imbalance is systemic, not isolated to one site or line.
- **Seasonality is real and predictable**: Dec–Feb consistently outpaces Jun–Aug, which means a
  pre-winter readiness program is a low-risk, high-confidence intervention.
- **243 events remain Pending.** Left untriaged, a growing pending backlog compounds resolution
  time and risks becoming a hidden cost center.

## Recommendations

1. **Shift maintenance mix toward Predictive/Preventive** for the top 10 highest-cost machines,
   starting with MC-0003. Condition monitoring (vibration, temperature, cycle-count sensors) on
   injection molding and stamping press assets specifically, since these carry outsized repair
   costs relative to event count.
2. **Renegotiate supplier SLAs and revisit buffer-stock policy** for the components behind
   Material Shortage events — this is the #1 cost category and is a supply-chain fix, not a
   maintenance one.
3. **Run a changeover time study (SMED methodology)** on Product Changeover, the most frequently
   logged reason, to cut planned downtime hours without new capital spend.
4. **Prioritize Plant Munich for a root-cause and staffing review**, given it carries the largest
   share of total downtime hours of the four plants across the two-year window.
5. **Clear the pending backlog** (243 events) with a weekly maintenance-triage cadence to prevent
   recurrence and protect the resolution-time KPI from drifting further.
6. **Build a winter-readiness checklist** (run each October–November) targeting the mechanical
   and electrical failure modes that spike December–February — pre-emptive lubrication,
   insulation checks, and backup power testing ahead of the cold season.

## Suggested next-quarter targets

- Reduce unplanned downtime share from 72.5% → 60% by shifting Material Shortage and
  Mechanical Failure events into scheduled/preventive categories.
- Cut MC-0003's repair cost run-rate by 25% via condition monitoring before considering
  full replacement.
- Reduce average resolution time from 2.02 hrs → 1.5 hrs by clearing the pending backlog and
  adding a triage SLA.
