EXECUTIVE INSIGHTS

Key findings, root causes & recommendations · Jan 2023 – Dec 2024
Executive Overview Executive Insights

Across 4,200 logged downtime events and 4,330 downtime hours over two years, unplanned failures account for 72.5% of all events and drove €2.05M in repair spend. Plant Munich is the largest single contributor to downtime hours, and material shortages — not mechanical wear — are the single costliest downtime category. The pattern points to a supply-chain and preventive-maintenance opportunity rather than a machine-reliability crisis alone.

Root cause summary

  • Material shortage is the single largest cost driver at €484.7k — ahead of mechanical failure (€362.3k) and electrical failure (€253.4k) — suggesting supplier reliability and buffer-stock policy issues outweigh equipment condition as a cost lever.
  • Unplanned downtime share is consistently high across all four plants (71.4%–73.8%), so the imbalance is systemic rather than isolated to one site.
  • Seasonality is real: winter months (Dec–Feb) run 15–25% higher downtime hours, consistent with cold-start mechanical stress and higher electrical fault rates.
  • 243 events remain in Pending status — a backlog that risks compounding if not resourced.

Recommendations

  1. Shift maintenance mix toward Predictive/Preventive for the top 10 highest-cost machines (led by MC-0003) — condition monitoring on injection molding and stamping press assets specifically.
  2. Renegotiate supplier SLAs / buffer stock for the components behind Material Shortage events, since this is the #1 cost category despite not being a machine reliability issue.
  3. Run a changeover time study (SMED) on Product Changeover — the most frequent reason — to cut planned downtime without new capital spend.
  4. Prioritize Plant Munich for a root-cause deep dive given it carries the largest share of total downtime hours across the two-year window.
  5. Clear the pending backlog (243 events) with a weekly triage cadence to prevent recurrence and protect resolution-time KPIs.
  6. Build a winter readiness checklist (Oct–Nov) targeting the mechanical/electrical failure modes that spike Dec–Feb.
